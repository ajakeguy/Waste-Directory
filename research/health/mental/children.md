## Short-Form Video Impact on Toddler Brain Development

**Core Idea:** Short-form video content (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) has a cocaine-like neurological impact on toddlers' developing brains due to:
- Prefrontal cortex barely developing in early childhood
- Dopamine sensitivity orders of magnitude higher than adults
- Neural pathways being wired in real-time
- Zero executive function to self-regulate consumption
- Irreversible compromise to brain development before maturity

**Key Claim:** Addiction to short-form videos reduces frontal lobe activity, weakening the ability to focus — comparable to giving cocaine to toddlers in terms of reward pathway hijacking.
